IFRS 9 Domain Accuracy Rules (DA-1 to DA-6)

Six domain-specific validation rules that enforce IFRS 9 mathematical and economic constraints on ECL inputs and outputs. These rules go beyond generic data quality checks to ensure the calculation engine produces results that are compliant with the standard.

Overview

Domain Accuracy (DA) rules form a fourth validation category alongside the existing Data Integrity, Model Reasonableness, and Governance rules. While the existing categories catch general data errors and process violations, DA rules enforce constraints that are specific to the IFRS 9 impairment model and its underlying credit risk mathematics.

DA rules are executed as part of the pre-calculation validation gate. If any CRITICAL-severity DA rule fails, the ECL calculation is blocked until the issue is resolved.

Detailed Rule Descriptions

DA-1: EAD Non-Negative (IFRS 9.B5.5.31)

Exposure at Default (EAD) is a core component of the ECL formula: ECL = PD x LGD x EAD x DF. IFRS 9.B5.5.31 defines EAD as the gross carrying amount of a financial asset plus any undrawn commitment amount at the time of default. Since both components are non-negative, EAD itself must be non-negative.

This is a Critical severity rule. A negative EAD would produce a negative ECL (a credit gain), which is economically meaningless and violates the loss allowance concept.

# Validation logic
violations = [v for v in ead_values if v < 0]
passed = len(violations) == 0

DA-2: LGD Unit Interval (IFRS 9.B5.5.28)

Loss Given Default represents the proportion of exposure that is lost when a borrower defaults. Per IFRS 9.B5.5.28, LGD must account for the recovery of collateral and other credit enhancements. The result is always a proportion:

  • LGD = 0 means full recovery (no loss on default)
  • LGD = 1 means total loss (no recovery)
  • Values outside [0, 1] are mathematically invalid

This rule is distinct from the Data Integrity rule D-3 (which checks the same range) because DA-2 is specifically tied to the IFRS 9 definition and runs as part of the domain accuracy category. Both rules must pass.

DA-3: Stage 3 PD Consistency (IFRS 9.B5.5.37)

Under IFRS 9, Stage 3 loans are credit-impaired. The definition of credit impairment in Appendix A includes events such as significant financial difficulty, breach of contract, or the borrower entering bankruptcy. For loans already in default, the probability of default is near-certain.

This rule checks that all Stage 3 loans have a PD of at least 0.90. A lower PD would be inconsistent with the credit-impaired classification. The 0.90 threshold (rather than 1.0) allows for partial cure expectations where the borrower may recover from default.

Cure Rate Consideration If your institution has a documented cure rate policy allowing Stage 3 loans to have PDs below 0.90, you may need to adjust the min_stage3_pd threshold in the validation configuration. Document the rationale for audit purposes.

DA-4: Discount Rate Validity (IFRS 9.B5.5.44)

IFRS 9.5.5.17 requires that ECL is measured as the present value of expected cash shortfalls, discounted at the Effective Interest Rate (EIR). The discount factor formula is:

DF = 1 / (1 + EIR) ^ t

For this formula to produce a valid, positive result, EIR must be strictly greater than -1 (i.e., greater than -100%). At EIR = -1, the denominator becomes zero, creating a division-by-zero error. This is a mathematical constraint rather than an economic one, but it is critical to prevent calculation failures.

DA-5: ECL Non-Negative (IFRS 9.5.5.1)

IFRS 9.5.5.1 states that an entity shall recognize a loss allowance for expected credit losses. The term "loss" inherently implies a non-negative value. A negative ECL would mean the entity expects to gain from credit risk, which contradicts the impairment model.

This rule runs as a post-calculation check, verifying that the ECL engine has not produced any negative values through rounding errors, misconfigured parameters, or bugs in the simulation logic.

DA-6: Minimum Scenario Count (IFRS 9.B5.5.42)

IFRS 9.B5.5.42 explicitly requires that the measurement of expected credit losses shall reflect "an unbiased and probability-weighted amount that is determined by evaluating a range of possible outcomes." The standard guidance recommends at least three scenarios:

  • Base — the most likely economic outcome
  • Upside — a more favorable economic outcome
  • Downside — an adverse economic outcome

Using fewer than three scenarios fails to capture the non-linear relationship between macroeconomic conditions and credit losses, which is a core requirement of the forward-looking ECL model.

Integration with the ECL Workflow

DA rules are integrated into the standard validation pipeline. They run alongside other pre-calculation rules when a project advances through the ECL workflow:

# Pre-calculation gate includes DA rules
results = run_all_pre_calculation_checks(
    scenario_weights=weights,
    pd_values=pds,
    lgd_values=lgds,
    eir_values=eirs,
    remaining_months=months,
    gca_values=gcas,
    ead_values=eads,          # DA-1: EAD non-negative
    stage_pd_pairs=pairs,     # DA-3: Stage 3 PD consistency
)
# DA-2 (LGD interval), DA-4 (discount rate), DA-6 (scenario count)
# are also evaluated automatically

DA-5 (ECL non-negative) runs as a post-calculation check and can be called independently after the Monte Carlo simulation completes.
