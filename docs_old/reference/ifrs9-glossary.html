IFRS 9 Glossary

Key IFRS 9 terms and concepts used throughout the application.

TermDefinitionApp Feature
12-Month ECL The portion of lifetime ECL that represents the expected credit losses resulting from default events that are possible within 12 months after the reporting date. Applied to Stage 1 financial assets. ECL Calculation
AUC (Area Under ROC Curve) A discrimination metric measuring a model's ability to distinguish between defaulters and non-defaulters. Values range from 0.5 (random) to 1.0 (perfect). Green threshold: ≥ 0.70. Backtesting
Backtesting The process of comparing model predictions with actual outcomes to assess model performance. Uses a traffic-light framework (Green/Amber/Red) based on multiple statistical metrics. Backtesting
Brier Score A calibration metric measuring the mean squared difference between predicted probabilities and actual outcomes. Lower is better. Green threshold: ≤ 0.15. Backtesting
CCF (Credit Conversion Factor) The ratio of the additional amount drawn down at default to the undrawn commitment at the observation date. Used to estimate EAD for revolving facilities. Advanced Features
Champion Model The currently active production model for a given model type and product. Only one champion per type/product combination. Other models serve as challengers. Model Registry
Collateral Haircut A reduction applied to the market value of collateral to account for potential decline in value during the time needed to realize the collateral. Expressed as a percentage. Advanced Features
Concordance Index (C-Index) A measure of discrimination for survival models. Represents the probability that for a randomly selected pair of subjects, the one who defaulted first had a higher predicted risk. Range: 0.5–1.0. Hazard Models
Cox Proportional Hazards A semi-parametric survival model that estimates the effect of covariates on the hazard (default) rate without specifying the baseline hazard function. Assumes proportional hazards. Hazard Models
Credit-Impaired A financial asset is credit-impaired when one or more events that have a detrimental impact on estimated future cash flows have occurred (e.g., significant financial difficulty, breach of contract, bankruptcy). Corresponds to Stage 3. ECL Workflow
Cure Rate The probability that a loan in Stage 2 or Stage 3 returns to Stage 1 (performing) status. Used to adjust ECL estimates for expected recoveries. Advanced Features
DPD (Days Past Due) The number of days a borrower is overdue on a payment. A key indicator for stage assignment: <30 DPD typically Stage 1, 30–89 DPD Stage 2, ≥90 DPD Stage 3. Data Review
EAD (Exposure at Default) The estimated amount of exposure at the time of default. For term loans, typically equals the outstanding balance. For revolving facilities, includes estimated future drawdowns (via CCF). ECL Calculation
ECL (Expected Credit Loss) The probability-weighted estimate of credit losses over the expected life of a financial instrument. Calculated as PD × LGD × EAD, adjusted for time value of money and scenario weighting. ECL Calculation
GCA (Gross Carrying Amount) The amortized cost of a financial asset before adjusting for any loss allowance. Represents the outstanding principal and accrued interest. Data Review
Gini Coefficient A measure of model discrimination derived from the AUC: Gini = 2 × AUC − 1. Ranges from 0 (no discrimination) to 1 (perfect). Green threshold: ≥ 0.40. Backtesting
IFRS 7 International Financial Reporting Standard 7 — Financial Instruments: Disclosures. Requires entities to provide disclosures about the significance of financial instruments, including credit risk exposure, loss allowance reconciliation, and sensitivity analysis. Regulatory Reports
IFRS 9 International Financial Reporting Standard 9 — Financial Instruments. Replaced IAS 39 and introduced the expected credit loss impairment model, replacing the incurred loss model. Effective from January 1, 2018. All
Kaplan-Meier Estimator A non-parametric survival estimator that computes the survival function from observed time-to-event data. Does not assume any distributional form for the hazard function. Hazard Models
KS (Kolmogorov-Smirnov) Statistic The maximum separation between the cumulative distribution functions of defaulters and non-defaulters. Measures model discrimination. Green threshold: ≥ 0.30. Backtesting
LGD (Loss Given Default) The proportion of exposure that is lost when a borrower defaults, after accounting for recoveries and collateral. Expressed as a percentage (0–100%) or decimal (0–1). ECL Calculation
Lifetime ECL The expected credit losses that result from all possible default events over the expected life of a financial instrument. Applied to Stage 2 and Stage 3 assets. ECL Calculation
Loss Allowance The provision (contra-asset) recognized on the balance sheet for expected credit losses. Equals the 12-month ECL for Stage 1 assets and lifetime ECL for Stage 2/3 assets. GL Journals
Management Overlay A post-model adjustment applied to ECL to account for factors not captured by the models, such as emerging risks, sector-specific concerns, or expert judgment. Must be documented and justified per IFRS 9.5.5.17(c). ECL Workflow (Step 5)
Markov Chain A stochastic model describing a sequence of possible events where the probability of each event depends only on the state attained in the previous event. Used for stage transition modeling. Markov Chains
Monte Carlo Simulation A computational technique that uses random sampling to obtain numerical results. In ECL, used to simulate multiple possible outcomes for PD and LGD to estimate the distribution of credit losses. Simulation
PD (Probability of Default) The likelihood that a borrower will default on their obligations within a specified time horizon. 12-month PD is used for Stage 1; lifetime PD for Stage 2/3. ECL Calculation
PSI (Population Stability Index) A metric measuring the shift in the distribution of a variable between two time periods. Used to detect model drift. Lower is better. Green threshold: ≤ 0.10. Backtesting
Satellite Model A regression model that links macroeconomic variables to credit risk parameters (PD, LGD). Used to project risk parameters under different economic scenarios. Model Execution
Scenario Weighting The process of assigning probability weights to different macroeconomic scenarios (e.g., base 50%, upside 20%, downside 30%) to compute the probability-weighted ECL per IFRS 9.5.5.17(a). ECL Workflow (Step 4)
SICR (Significant Increase in Credit Risk) The threshold for transferring a financial asset from Stage 1 to Stage 2. Assessed by comparing the risk of default at the reporting date with the risk at initial recognition. A 30-day past due rebuttable presumption applies. Stage Assignment
Stage 1 Financial assets where credit risk has not increased significantly since initial recognition. Loss allowance equals 12-month ECL. Interest revenue calculated on gross carrying amount. ECL Workflow
Stage 2 Financial assets where credit risk has increased significantly since initial recognition but are not credit-impaired. Loss allowance equals lifetime ECL. Interest revenue calculated on gross carrying amount. ECL Workflow
Stage 3 Credit-impaired financial assets. Loss allowance equals lifetime ECL. Interest revenue calculated on the net carrying amount (gross amount minus loss allowance). ECL Workflow
Stage Migration The movement of financial assets between IFRS 9 stages (1→2, 2→3, 3→2, etc.) during a reporting period. Tracked in a transition matrix for disclosure and analysis. Regulatory Reports
Survival Curve A function showing the probability that a borrower survives (does not default) beyond a given time point. Derived from hazard models. S(t) = P(T > t). Hazard Models
Traffic-Light Framework A backtesting methodology that classifies model performance as Green (acceptable), Amber (needs monitoring), or Red (requires action) based on statistical thresholds for each metric. Backtesting
Transition Matrix A square matrix where each element (i,j) represents the probability of transitioning from state i to state j over a given period. Used in Markov chain analysis for stage migration modeling. Markov Chains
Vintage Analysis Analysis of loan performance grouped by origination period (vintage). Helps identify whether credit quality is improving or deteriorating across origination cohorts. Data Review, Stress Testing
Waterfall (Attribution) A chart showing the decomposition of ECL movement from opening to closing balance, broken down by driver: new originations, derecognitions, stage transfers, model changes, macro changes, overlays, write-offs, and other factors. Attribution
Write-Off The removal of a financial asset from the balance sheet when there is no reasonable expectation of recovery. The gross carrying amount is reduced, and any loss allowance previously recognized is also derecognized. GL Journals
